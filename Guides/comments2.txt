For your current notebook, these are the highest ROI improvements in order:

1. Time Features (Highest Impact)

Right now you're feeding timestamp almost directly.

Add:

hour
minute
is_morning_peak (7-10)
is_evening_peak (5-8)

and

sin_time
cos_time

The Grab winners found hour to be one of the most important features. Traffic is highly dependent on time of day.

2. Decode Geohash

Currently:

geohash -> LabelEncoder

Better:

geohash -> latitude
geohash -> longitude

Then keep:

lat
lon

as numerical features.

This was used in top Grab solutions and usually improves performance because nearby locations get similar representations.

3. Geo Clustering

After decoding geohash:

KMeans(n_clusters=20)

Create:

geo_cluster

feature.

Grab finalists reported this among their most useful features.

4. Feature Crosses

Create:

RoadType + Weather
RoadType + LargeVehicles
NumberOfLanes * LargeVehicles

Example:

Residential_Rainy
Street_Sunny

Traffic behaves very differently under these combinations.

5. CatBoost

Try:

CatBoostRegressor

alongside LightGBM.

For datasets with many categorical columns:

geohash
RoadType
Weather
Landmarks
LargeVehicles

CatBoost often performs surprisingly well.

6. Ensemble

Instead of:

Final = LightGBM

do:

Final = 0.5*LightGBM + 0.5*CatBoost

This was a common pattern in Grab solutions.

7. Proper Validation

Your notebook appears to use a normal split.

Use:

TimeSeriesSplit

or

train = earlier timestamps
valid = later timestamps

Traffic data is temporal.

8. Feature Importance Plot

For presentation:

Show:

Top Features
-------------
hour
geohash
temperature
weather
lanes

Judges love interpretability.

The old Gridlock challenge was vulnerable to a simple lookup-table solution that achieved near-perfect scores. If your current hackathon dataset has a similar pattern, that can outperform any ML model.
